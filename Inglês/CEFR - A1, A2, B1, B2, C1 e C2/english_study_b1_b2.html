01 B1 Core

Present Perfect

have/has + past participle — connecting past to present

Structure & Usage

Subject + have/has + past participle (+ object)
  • Experience: I have visited Japan. (at some point in life)
  • Unfinished time: She has worked here for ten years. (still working)
  • Recent events with present relevance: He has just left. / I've lost my keys.
  • Key words: ever, never, already, yet, just, for, since, recently

Present Perfect vs. Past Simple — Key Contrast

Present PerfectPast Simple
I have seen that film.I saw that film yesterday.
Unspecified / irrelevant timeSpecific time in the past
Have you ever been to Rome?Did you go to Rome last year?
She has lived here for 3 years.She lived in Paris for 3 years (but not anymore).

02 B1 Core

Past Perfect

had + past participle — the "past of the past"

Structure & Usage

Subject + had + past participle
  • Use when one past action happened before another past action.
  • When I arrived, the meeting had already started. (Meeting = earlier past)
  • Common with: after, before, when, by the time, already, just, never
  • Negative: hadn't (had not) + past participle

Future Forms

will / going to / Present Continuous / Future Perfect

Four Key Future Forms

FormUseExample
will + baseDecisions at moment of speaking, predictions, offersI'll help you with that.
going to + basePlans & intentions already decided, evidence-based predictionsLook at those clouds — it's going to rain.
Pres. ContinuousFixed arrangements in the near futureI'm meeting her at noon.
will + have + ppFuture Perfect: completed before a future pointBy 2030, they will have finished the project.

05 B1–B2

Modal Verbs

Ability, permission, obligation, deduction & possibility

Key Modals at B1–B2

ModalFunctionExample
can / couldAbility, possibility, permissionShe can speak Arabic. Could I sit here?
must / have toStrong obligation (must = internal; have to = external)I must call her. You have to wear a helmet.
should / ought toAdvice, recommendationYou should see a doctor.
might / mayPossibility (might = less certain)It might rain. She may be right.
must / can'tLogical deduction (positive / negative)He must be tired. That can't be true.
needn't / don't have toLack of necessityYou needn't hurry.

06 B1–B2

Passive Voice

be + past participle — focusing on the action, not the doer

Structure Across Tenses

TensePassive Form
Present Simpleis/are + pp: The car is washed daily.
Past Simplewas/were + pp: The letter was sent yesterday.
Present Perfecthas/have been + pp: The report has been submitted.
Future (will)will be + pp: The bridge will be built by 2030.
Modalmodal + be + pp: The form must be filled in.

Use by to introduce the agent when relevant: The novel was written by Tolkien.

07 B1–B2

Reported Speech

Backshift tenses, pronoun changes, time expressions

Tense Backshift Rules

Direct SpeechReported Speech
Present Simple: "I work here."Past Simple: She said she worked there.
Past Simple: "I went to Paris."Past Perfect: He said he had gone to Paris.
Present Perfect: "I've seen it."Past Perfect: She said she had seen it.
will: "I will call."would: He said he would call.
can: "I can help."could: She said she could help.

Time Expression Changes

  • now → then | today → that day | tomorrow → the next day
  • yesterday → the day before | last week → the week before
  • here → there | this → that | these → those

08 B1–B2

Relative Clauses

who, which, that, whose, where, when — defining & non-defining

Relative Pronouns

PronounRefers toExample
who / thatPeopleThe woman who called is my sister.
which / thatThings / animalsThe book which I bought is great.
whosePossession (people & things)The student whose work won the prize…
wherePlaceThe city where I grew up is beautiful.
whenTimeThe year when she was born…

Defining vs. Non-defining

  • Defining: no commas — essential information: The man who called me is my boss.
  • Non-defining: commas — extra information, cannot use "that": My boss, who is very kind, called me.

09 B1–B2

Gerunds & Infinitives

When to use verb + -ing vs. verb + to + base

Key Patterns

  • Gerund (-ing) after: enjoy, avoid, suggest, consider, finish, mind, keep, practice, admit, deny, risk, miss
  • Infinitive (to + base) after: want, need, decide, hope, plan, manage, seem, afford, agree, refuse, offer, promise, expect
  • Both (with meaning change): stop, remember, forget, try, regret
I stopped smoking. (= I quit) | I stopped to smoke. (= I stopped doing sth else in order to smoke)
