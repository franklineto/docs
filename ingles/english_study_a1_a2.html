01A1 Core

Verb To Be

am / is / are — the most essential verb in English

Structure & Conjugation

I → am  |  He / She / It → is  |  You / We / They → are
  • Negative: add not → I am not (I'm not), he is not (isn't), they are not (aren't).
  • Questions: invert subject and verb → Are you happy? / Is she a teacher?
  • Short answers: Yes, I am. / No, she isn't. / Yes, they are.

Examples

English
I am a student.
Português
Eu sou estudante.
English
She is not at home.
Português
Ela não está em casa.
English
Are they your friends?
Português
Eles são seus amigos?

02A1 Core

Articles

a / an / the — definite and indefinite articles

Grammar Rule

  • a — before consonant sounds: a car, a book, a university
  • an — before vowel sounds: an apple, an hour, an honest man
  • the — specific or unique nouns: the sun, the book I gave you
  • no article — general plurals & uncountable: I like music / Dogs are friendly
KEY: It's the SOUND, not the letter! "hour" → /aʊər/ → an hour ✓

04A1 Core

Personal Pronouns

Subject & object pronouns

Subject vs. Object Pronouns

Subject: I · you · he · she · it · we · they
Object: me · you · him · her · it · us · them
  • Subject pronouns come before the verb: She loves coffee.
  • Object pronouns come after the verb or preposition: Give it to me.

05A1–A2

Present Simple

Habits, routines, facts and general truths

Structure

I/You/We/They + verb base  |  He/She/It + verb + s/es
  • Negative: do/does + not + verb base → She doesn't like coffee.
  • Question: Do/Does + subject + verb? → Does he work here?
  • Special spellings: go → goes, study → studies, watch → watches

06A2 Core

Present Continuous

Actions happening right now or temporary situations

Structure

Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing
  • Spelling: run → running  |  sit → sitting  |  make → making
  • Negative: am/is/are + not + verb-ing
  • Question: Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?
  • Signal words: now, right now, at the moment, look!

07A1–A2

There is / There are

Describing existence and quantity

Structure

There is + singular noun  |  There are + plural noun
  • Negative: There isn't / There aren't
  • Question: Is there…? / Are there…?

09A1–A2

Adjectives

Description, comparatives and superlatives

Adjective Rules

Adjective + noun  |  Subject + to be + adjective
  • Adjectives do not change for plural: a big house / big houses
  • Order: Opinion → Size → Age → Color → Origin → Noun
  • Comparative: adj + -er / more + adj: taller, more interesting
  • Superlative: the + adj + -est / the most + adj: the tallest, the most beautiful
  • Irregulars: good → better → the best  |  bad → worse → the worst

10A2 Core

Past Simple

Completed actions in the past

Structure

Regular: verb + -ed  |  Irregular: go→went, eat→ate, see→saw…
  • Negative: did not (didn't) + verb base
  • Question: Did + subject + verb base?
  • Common irregulars: have→had, come→came, buy→bought, take→took, wake→woke
